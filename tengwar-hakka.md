# Tengwar Mode for Hakka

## Why this mode exists

Hakka is the third Sinitic language to receive a Tengwar mode, following Mandarin and Cantonese. This mode builds on the Cantonese infrastructure, inheriting approximately 90% of its mappings. Hakka shares Cantonese's preservation of final stops and six-tone structure, but differs in consonant inventory and tone contour values.

Primary dialect: **Sixian** (four counties), the most widely spoken Taiwanese Hakka variety. Optional extension for Hailu (sea and land) dialect noted where relevant.

This is a phonemic tehtar mode. It uses the Taiwanese Hakka Romanization System (Taiwan MOE) as its reference.

## Romanization reference

This mode uses the **Taiwanese Hakka Romanization System** published by Taiwan's Ministry of Education (2012) as its primary reference.

Key features:
- Aspirated consonants: p, t, k, c, q (vs unaspirated b, d, g, z, j)
- Apical vowel: ii (syllabic alveolar approximant)
- Palatal affricates: j, q, x
- Tones: superscript numbers 1-6

Example: ngai (I/me), vuk (house)

### Hakka-specific initials (3 initials)

| Hakka | IPA | Tengwa | Rationale |
|-------|-----|--------|-----------|
| v | /v/ | ampa | Grade 4 (voiced fricative) in labial series; mirrors formen:ampa as /f/:/v/ |
| j | /tc/ | quessë + palatal mark | Reuses Mandarin palatal convention |
| q | /tch/ | ungwë + palatal mark | Reuses Mandarin palatal convention |
| x | /c/ | harma + palatal mark | Reuses Mandarin palatal convention |

**The /v/ initial:** Hakka preserves /v/ from Middle Chinese *m- before certain rimes where Cantonese has /m/ or /w/. Examples: vu (martial), vuk (house), vun (writing).

**Palatal affricates:** Sixian has distinct /tc, tch, c/ that Cantonese lacks but Mandarin has. These use the Mandarin convention: velar tengwar plus below-tengwa palatal diacritic.

### What Hakka lacks

- Labiovelar gw/kw (Cantonese only)
- Retroflex zh/ch/sh/r (Mandarin only)

### Hailu extension (optional)

Hailu preserves postalveolar consonants that Sixian merges as alveolars:

| Hailu | IPA | Tengwa | Notes |
|-------|-----|--------|-------|
| zh | /ts`/ | calma | Reuses Mandarin retroflex position |
| ch | /ts`h/ | anga | Reuses Mandarin retroflex position |
| sh | /s`/ | hwesta | Reuses Mandarin retroflex position |
| rh | /z`/ | órë | Voiced counterpart |

## Vowels

Five of six Hakka vowels inherit directly from Cantonese.

### Inherited from Cantonese (5 vowels)

| Hakka | IPA | Tehta |
|-------|-----|-------|
| a | /a/ | a-tehta (three dots) |
| e | /E/ | e-tehta (acute) |
| i | /i/ | i-tehta (single dot) |
| o | /O/ | o-tehta (left curl) |
| u | /u/ | u-tehta (right curl) |

### Hakka-specific vowel (1 vowel)

| Hakka | IPA | Tehta | Context |
|-------|-----|-------|---------|
| ii | /1/ ~ /r=/ | unutixe (syllabic mark) | After z, c, s only |

**Apical vowel:** The syllabic alveolar approximant romanized "ii" occurs only after alveolar consonants. This is the same phoneme as Mandarin's apical vowel in zi, ci, si. The unutixe mark indicates a consonant functioning as syllable nucleus.

### Diphthongs

All Hakka diphthongs follow the Cantonese pattern: tehta marks the nuclear vowel, glides are predictable from structure.

| Hakka | IPA | Tehta |
|-------|-----|-------|
| ai | /ai/ | a-tehta |
| au | /au/ | a-tehta |
| eu | /Eu/ | e-tehta |
| oi | /Oi/ | o-tehta |
| ia | /ia/ | a-tehta (with anna glide) |
| ie | /ie/ | e-tehta (with anna glide) |
| io | /iO/ | o-tehta (with anna glide) |
| iu | /iu/ | u-tehta (with anna glide) |
| ui | /ui/ | i-tehta (with vala glide) |
| iau | /iau/ | a-tehta (with anna glide) |

### What Hakka lacks

- Phonemic length contrast (Cantonese aa vs a)
- Short central /6/ (Cantonese underdot)
- Front rounded /9/ (Cantonese oe)
- Central rounded /8/ (Cantonese eo)
- Syllabic nasals (Cantonese m, ng)

## Final consonants

Hakka preserves all six Middle Chinese finals, identical to Cantonese. The same tengwar serve as both initials and finals; position distinguishes them.

### Nasal finals

| Final | IPA | Tengwa |
|-------|-----|--------|
| -m | /m/ | malta |
| -n | /n/ | numen |
| -ng | /ng/ | noldo |

### Stop finals (unreleased)

| Final | IPA | Tengwa |
|-------|-----|--------|
| -p | /p-/ | parma |
| -t | /t-/ | tinco |
| -k | /k-/ | calma |

Final stops use Grade 1 (unaspirated) tengwar. The unreleased quality is phonetic detail, not marked separately.

## Tones

Sixian has six tones with different Chao contour values from Cantonese. The mode uses the same extended tone system: iconographic contours plus register modifiers.

### Sixian tone inventory

| Tone | Category | Chao | Description | Mark |
|------|----------|------|-------------|------|
| 1 | Yin Ping | 24 | mid-rising | rising stroke |
| 2 | Yang Ping | 11 | low level | flat bar + double-dot |
| 3 | Shang Sheng | 31 | mid-falling | falling stroke + dot |
| 4 | Qu Sheng | 55 | high level | flat bar |
| 5 | Yin Ru | 2 | low checked | flat bar + double-dot |
| 6 | Yang Ru | 5 | high checked | flat bar |

### Rationale for tone assignments

The extended system maps contour shape and register independently:

- **Tone 1 (24):** Rising contour, mid starting point. Rising stroke alone.
- **Tone 2 (11):** Level contour at low register. Flat bar + double-dot.
- **Tone 3 (31):** Falling contour at mid register. Falling stroke + single dot.
- **Tone 4 (55):** Level contour at high register. Flat bar alone.
- **Tones 5/6 (checked):** Final stop implies checked quality. Pitch marks follow the same logic: low (T5) uses double-dot, high (T6) uses plain bar.

### Tone mark codepoints

| Mark | Composition | Codepoints |
|------|-------------|------------|
| Rising stroke | acute-teng | U+E046 |
| Flat bar | nasalizer-teng | U+E050 |
| Falling stroke | grave-teng | U+E054 |
| Single dot below | unutixe-teng | U+E045 |
| Double dot below | dotdblbelow-teng | U+E043 |

Tone marks appear below the tengwa bearing the nuclear vowel, same as Cantonese.

### Hailu extension (7 tones)

Hailu splits the Qu category into Yin and Yang, yielding a 7th tone:

| Tone | Category | Chao | Mark |
|------|----------|------|------|
| 5 | Yang Qu | 33 | flat bar + dot |

## Syllable structure

Hakka follows the standard Sinitic template, identical to Cantonese:

```
(Initial) (Medial) Nucleus (Coda) Tone
```

Maximum complexity: CGVC (e.g., kiang /kiang/)

## Comparison with Cantonese mode

| Feature | Cantonese | Hakka | Delta |
|---------|-----------|-------|-------|
| /v/ initial | absent | present | +1 tengwa (ampa) |
| Palatals j/q/x | absent | present | reuse Mandarin marks |
| Apical vowel /1/ | absent | present | +1 tehta (unutixe) |
| Labialized gw/kw | present | absent | not needed |
| Short vowels /6/, /8/, /9/ | present | absent | not needed |
| Vowel length contrast | present | absent | not needed |
| Syllabic nasals | present | absent | not needed |
| Tone count | 6 | 6 | same |
| Tone contours | different | different | same marks, different assignments |
| Final stops | -p, -t, -k | -p, -t, -k | identical |
| Final nasals | -m, -n, -ng | -m, -n, -ng | identical |

## Font requirements

Hakka requires no new glyphs beyond Cantonese and Mandarin modes:

- **ampa** (for /v/): Standard tengwa, in Alcarin
- **palatal mark**: From Mandarin mode
- **unutixe**: Standard syllabic marker, in Alcarin
- **extended tone marks**: From Cantonese mode
