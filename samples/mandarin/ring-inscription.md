## Full Ring-Verse

The complete poem, of which the Ring inscription is the final stanza:

| Chinese | English |
|---------|---------|
| 天下精灵铸三戒 | Three Rings for the Elven-kings under the sky |
| 地底矮人得七戒 | Seven for the Dwarf-lords in their halls of stone |
| 寿定凡人持九戒 | Nine for Mortal Men doomed to die |
| 魔多妖境暗影伏 | One for the Dark Lord on his dark throne |
| 暗王坐拥至尊戒 | In the Land of Mordor where the Shadows lie |
| 至尊戒，驭众戒 | One Ring to rule them all |
| 至尊戒，寻众戒 | One Ring to find them |
| 魔戒至尊引众戒 | One Ring to bring them all |
| 禁锢众戒黑暗中 | And in the darkness bind them |
| 魔多妖境暗影伏 | In the Land of Mordor where the Shadows lie |

## Notes

The Chinese translation renders the parallelism of the original elegantly:
- 至尊戒 (zhì zūn jiè) = "supreme ring" / "sovereign ring"
- 驭 (yù) = "to control, to drive" → rule
- 寻 (xún) = "to seek, to find" → find
- 引 (yǐn) = "to lead, to draw" → bring
- 禁锢 (jìn gù) = "to imprison, to shackle" → bind
- 黑暗中 (hēi àn zhōng) = "in the darkness"
